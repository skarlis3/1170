Awkward Sentences

“Sentence structure errors” refers to essentially a miscellaneous group of mistakes. Many teachers use “awkward” for these because the grammatical structure is hard to break down and explain. Basically, in this type of error, the writer has started writing one sentence structure and then abruptly switches to another. Here’s an example:

A trip to Japan had been his plan to visit the Far East was finally coming true.

With this type of error, the writer has most likely stopped in the middle of writing a sentence to think of the ending idea or what words to use. But when the writer gets back to the sentence, the initial structure has been forgotten, and the writer starts in with a new structure.

We can’t point to specific rules for all of these, but we can edit for them fairly easily. Read the sentence aloud, listening for the sense of it. When the sentence “falls apart,” you know that you need to revise that sentence. The one above could be revised in several ways, such as these:

A trip to Japan had been his plan. Visiting the Far East was finally coming true.

His plan to visit the Far East with a trip to Japan was finally coming true.

His plan, visiting the Far East, was coming true through a trip to Japan.

The main issue here is that you need to carefully read over the sentences that you. A spelling and grammar check on the computer may not catch these errors.
